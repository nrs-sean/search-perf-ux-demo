UX takeaway

For server-backed typeahead, 150–250 ms is usually a good starting point. Past ~300 ms of debounce, users begin to feel the delay; with high server latency, even a “good” debounce may feel slow.

What this demonstrates iQualitative thresholds are based on modern responsiveness guidance: Google’s INP Core Web Vital rates ≤200 ms as good and >500 ms as poor, while mobile search latency research shows delays around 7–10 seconds significantly worsen perceived experience. Sources: Google INP thresholds, mobile search latency study, and classic NN/g response-time guidance.
